# Competition Layout Brief
## The Living Threads

---

## Purpose

This document defines the visual integration of the digital initiative into the existing documentary experience.

The objective is not to introduce a new chapter.

Instead, it establishes a quiet editorial frame that allows visitors to immediately understand what they are entering before the documentary begins.

The documentary itself remains unchanged.

---

# Design Objective

The framing should answer orientation, not narrative.

Visitors should understand:

• what this initiative is,

• why it exists,

• and how they should approach the experience.

Afterward, the framing disappears completely.

The documentary takes over.

---

# Relationship to Hero

The framing is not a standalone section.

It belongs to Hero.

Hero therefore consists of two consecutive moments:

Editorial Introduction

↓

Documentary Opening

The transition between them should feel continuous.

Visitors should not perceive that they have entered a new page or a different section.

---

# Information Hierarchy

Only four pieces of information should appear.

### Initiative

A small editorial overline identifying the initiative.

Example:

*"A Digital Initiative for Living Heritage"*

---

### Title

The Living Threads

This remains the visual anchor.

No alternative branding should compete with it.

---

### Context

One short paragraph introducing the purpose of the initiative.

The paragraph should explain the mission.

It should never summarize the documentary.

---

### Invitation

A single restrained sentence encouraging slow exploration.

Examples include:

*"Take your time."*

*"Scroll slowly."*

*"Begin the journey."*

The wording should remain observational rather than instructional.

---

# Layout

The framing should occupy only the upper portion of Hero.

It should not consume the visual dominance of the opening imagery.

Recommended structure:

Overline

↓

Title

↓

Short contextual paragraph

↓

Invitation

↓

Hero imagery

↓

Existing Hero narrative

The transition should feel like a single editorial spread.

---

# Typography

Hierarchy should follow existing project rules.

Overline

Smallest.

Muted.

Uppercase or small caps if appropriate.

Title

Largest typography on the page.

Unchanged from the existing Hero.

Context

Editorial body text.

Readable but restrained.

Invitation

Small.

Quiet.

Secondary emphasis.

Nothing should visually compete with the title.

---

# Visual Language

No additional colors.

No icons.

No badges.

No decorative illustrations.

No background panels.

No borders.

No containers.

The framing must inherit the existing editorial language entirely.

If the documentary avoids interface, the framing should avoid interface as well.

---

# Spacing

Whitespace communicates orientation.

The framing should breathe naturally into Hero.

Spacing should gradually decrease as the eye approaches the Hero imagery.

Nothing should feel separated by hard visual boundaries.

---

# Motion

The framing follows Hero's existing motion philosophy.

The overline appears first.

The title follows.

The contextual paragraph settles afterward.

The invitation appears last.

Immediately afterward, Hero continues using its existing choreography.

No additional animation language may be introduced.

---

# Competition Communication

The framing should communicate the following ideas without explicit explanation.

This is real.

This is contemporary.

This is a digital initiative.

This is about preserving living culture.

The visitor should understand these ideas naturally rather than reading them as promotional claims.

---

# What Must Never Happen

The framing must never resemble:

• a marketing hero

• a startup landing page

• a product announcement

• an NGO donation page

• an exhibition poster

• a campaign website

The visitor should feel welcomed into an editorial publication, not persuaded by advertising.

---

# Success Criteria

After reading only the introduction, visitors should understand:

This experience belongs to a digital initiative.

After finishing Hope, visitors should understand:

Why the initiative exists.

Neither understanding should require explicit explanation.

The documentary remains responsible for emotional persuasion.

The framing remains responsible only for orientation.
