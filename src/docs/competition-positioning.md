# Competition Positioning
## The Living Threads
### SDGs Creative Web Competition — Bytesfest 2026

---

## Purpose

This document defines how *The Living Threads* positions itself within the context of the SDGs Creative Web Competition.

Unlike the Design Intent, Story Freeze, Layout Briefs, and Motion Choreographies, this document does not describe how the experience should be designed.

Instead, it explains why the experience exists, how it answers the competition brief, and which strategic principles must remain protected during any competition-oriented refinement.

This document should be consulted before introducing any feature, copy, or interaction intended to improve competition alignment.

---

# Competition Context

The competition challenges participants to design a landing page for an SDG digital solution.

This distinction is important.

The objective is not to build a cultural website.

The objective is to communicate the identity, value, and impact of a digital initiative through an engaging landing page experience.

Therefore, every future adjustment must strengthen this positioning without compromising the editorial nature of the project.

---

# Positioning Statement

*The Living Threads* is the flagship storytelling experience of a digital cultural preservation initiative dedicated to Sumba Ikat.

The documentary is intentionally presented as the initiative's primary public-facing experience.

Rather than functioning as a conventional promotional website, it establishes identity through observation, atmosphere, and emotional understanding.

The website is therefore both:

- an editorial documentary, and
- the landing page representing a broader digital initiative.

These two identities must never compete.

They should reinforce one another.

---

# The Digital Solution

The documentary itself is not the complete solution.

Instead, it represents the first point of contact with a larger digital ecosystem whose mission is to:

- preserve cultural knowledge,
- strengthen appreciation for Sumba Ikat,
- connect younger generations with living traditions,
- increase public awareness through accessible digital experiences,
- encourage long-term cultural sustainability.

The website introduces this mission emotionally before explaining it rationally.

---

# Branding Philosophy

The project rejects conventional promotional language.

Visitors are never asked to care.

Instead, they are invited to observe.

Understanding comes before appreciation.

Appreciation comes before preservation.

For this reason, branding emerges from experience rather than repetition.

Identity is communicated through atmosphere, pacing, imagery, and narrative progression—not slogans or marketing copy.

---

# Relationship with SDG 11

The project directly supports Sustainable Development Goal 11:

"Sustainable Cities and Communities."

More specifically, it contributes to safeguarding intangible cultural heritage through contemporary digital storytelling.

The website does not attempt to archive culture.

Instead, it preserves cultural relevance.

Its purpose is to reconnect modern audiences with traditional knowledge in ways that feel emotionally meaningful and culturally respectful.

---

# Why This Is A Landing Page

Traditional landing pages optimize for immediate conversion.

The Living Threads optimizes for lasting transformation.

Instead of maximizing clicks, it maximizes understanding.

Instead of reducing attention spans, it intentionally slows them.

Instead of asking visitors to act immediately, it prepares them to care first.

The documentary therefore fulfills the fundamental purpose of a landing page—not by following common interface patterns, but by establishing a memorable identity that visitors continue carrying after leaving the website.

---

# Competition Alignment Principles

Future competition-oriented refinements should follow these principles.

## Preserve the Documentary

Editorial storytelling remains the primary experience.

The competition should never transform the project into a conventional promotional website.

---

## Strengthen Positioning

Competition alignment should clarify what the visitor is experiencing.

It should never require rewriting the narrative itself.

---

## Introduce Context, Not Marketing

Visitors may be gently informed that they are experiencing a digital cultural initiative.

They should never feel that they are being sold a product.

---

## Preserve Emotional Progression

The narrative progression—

Wonder

↓

Observation

↓

Creation

↓

Reflection

↓

Living Heritage

↓

Hope

—must remain completely intact.

Competition adjustments should support this progression rather than interrupt it.

---

## Maintain Editorial Restraint

Every additional interface element increases cognitive load.

Only additions that strengthen clarity without weakening atmosphere should be considered acceptable.

---

# Success Criteria

The project succeeds when visitors understand three things simultaneously.

First,

they understand that Sumba Ikat is a living cultural heritage rather than a historical artifact.

Second,

they recognize that this experience represents a digital initiative dedicated to preserving that heritage.

Third,

they leave remembering the emotional journey instead of the interface used to deliver it.

When these three conditions are achieved together, the project fulfills both its editorial ambitions and the objectives of the competition.

---

# Implementation Principle

Competition alignment should always be achieved through framing rather than expansion.

The documentary remains the product.

The branding exists to support the documentary.

Never the reverse.
