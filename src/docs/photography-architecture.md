# Photography Architecture

## Purpose

Photography within **The Living Threads** is not organized by chapters, but by narrative roles.

Each photograph exists to perform a specific storytelling function rather than simply illustrating surrounding text.

By defining visual roles before selecting images, the documentary maintains consistency even when photographs originate from different sources. Every selected image contributes to a larger visual language instead of existing as an isolated illustration.

This architecture establishes the relationship between story structure and photographic structure.

---

# Visual Roles

## Visual Role 01 — Invitation

### Narrative Purpose

Create curiosity.

The first photograph should encourage visitors to begin the journey rather than explain it.

It should reveal only enough information to invite exploration.

### Emotional Tone

Wonder.

Quiet.

Poetic.

### Typical Subject

- partial textile
- weaving detail
- hands beginning a process
- abstract natural texture

### Visual Characteristics

- generous negative space
- restrained composition
- minimal visual complexity
- soft natural light

---

## Visual Role 02 — Context

### Narrative Purpose

Introduce place.

These images establish where the story happens without becoming landscape photography.

Environment exists to support the craft.

### Emotional Tone

Observation.

Calm.

Grounded.

### Typical Subject

- traditional house
- weaving workspace
- village surroundings
- drying area
- natural environment

### Visual Characteristics

- environmental medium-wide
- layered depth
- subtle human traces
- balanced composition

---

## Visual Role 03 — Material

### Narrative Purpose

Reveal the origin of the textile.

Visitors should understand that every thread begins with natural materials.

### Emotional Tone

Discovery.

Organic.

Patient.

### Typical Subject

- cotton
- natural dyes
- leaves
- roots
- fibres
- yarn preparation

### Visual Characteristics

- close detail
- tactile texture
- natural colour
- soft light

---

## Visual Role 04 — Process

### Narrative Purpose

Show work in progress.

These photographs communicate that weaving is continuous physical labour rather than a finished object.

### Emotional Tone

Energy.

Rhythm.

Dedication.

### Typical Subject

- weaving
- tying
- dyeing
- spinning
- preparing tools

### Visual Characteristics

- visible movement
- layered hands
- repeated actions
- medium framing

Energy should emerge from gesture rather than dramatic camera techniques.

---

## Visual Role 05 — Gesture

### Narrative Purpose

Reveal human knowledge through physical movement.

Hands become visual evidence of experience, memory, and craftsmanship.

### Emotional Tone

Intimate.

Focused.

Respectful.

### Typical Subject

- hands weaving
- fingers tying threads
- holding tools
- preparing fibres

### Visual Characteristics

- close framing
- shallow visual complexity
- texture-driven composition
- minimal distractions

Faces are optional.

Hands are essential.

---

## Visual Role 06 — Meaning

### Narrative Purpose

Slow the narrative.

These images encourage reflection instead of explanation.

Photography becomes quieter and more symbolic.

### Emotional Tone

Reflection.

Memory.

Identity.

### Typical Subject

- finished motifs
- traditional patterns
- quiet portraits
- symbolic objects

### Visual Characteristics

- slower visual rhythm
- generous whitespace
- restrained composition
- subtle emotional presence

---

## Visual Role 07 — Continuity

### Narrative Purpose

Show that tradition survives because it is shared.

These photographs reveal relationships rather than individuals.

### Emotional Tone

Warm.

Human.

Enduring.

### Typical Subject

- multiple generations
- teaching moments
- family weaving
- community activity

### Visual Characteristics

- natural interaction
- environmental framing
- observational perspective
- balanced composition

The emphasis is on transmission rather than instruction.

---

## Visual Role 08 — Release

### Narrative Purpose

Allow the documentary to gradually disappear.

The final photographs should leave emotional space instead of delivering a conclusion.

### Emotional Tone

Hope.

Stillness.

Release.

### Typical Subject

- quiet textile detail
- empty workspace
- soft natural light
- distant figure
- open landscape
- woven cloth resting naturally

### Visual Characteristics

- minimal composition
- large negative space
- soft light
- restrained colour palette
- visual simplicity

The final image should feel like an ending without announcing that the story has ended.

# Narrative Flow

Each visual role supports one stage of the documentary experience.

Invitation

↓

Context

↓

Material

↓

Process

↓

Gesture

↓

Meaning

↓

Continuity

↓

Release

Although some roles may appear multiple times throughout the website, their emotional progression should remain consistent.

Visitors should experience a gradual movement from curiosity toward quiet reflection.

---

# Reuse Principles

Visual roles are reusable building blocks.

Multiple placeholders may share the same role if they communicate the same narrative purpose.

For example:

- several photographs may use **Gesture**
- multiple photographs may use **Process**
- only one photograph should function as **Invitation**
- only one photograph should function as **Release**

This prevents visual redundancy while preserving editorial rhythm.

---

# Success Criteria

The photography architecture is successful when every photograph can be identified by its narrative role rather than by its location on the page.

Removing chapter titles should not break the visual narrative.

Visitors should still perceive a coherent documentary journey through photography alone.
