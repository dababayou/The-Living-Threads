# Living Heritage — Layout Brief

Status: FROZEN

---

# Chapter Philosophy

## Core Principle

Living Heritage is the first chapter where people become the primary visual subject.

The chapter is not composed around textiles.

It is composed around relationships.

Every layout decision should strengthen the feeling that culture exists because people continue living it together.

---

## Spatial Philosophy

Shared Space.

Whitespace is not emptiness.

Whitespace represents the invisible space between people.

Every composition should feel inhabited,

even when large areas remain visually quiet.

---

## Camera Philosophy

Eye-level.

The visitor should never observe from above,

nor admire from below.

The camera behaves like another participant quietly sharing the same environment.

---

## Human Priority

Whenever a choice exists between emphasizing:

- textile

or

- human relationship

always prioritize the relationship.

The textile supports the people.

The people never support the textile.

---

## Reading Rhythm

Conversation.

Layouts should respond to one another,

rather than replace one another.

Each frame should feel like the next sentence in the same conversation.

---

## Overall Layout Direction

Visual Density

Medium–Low.

Higher than Meaning.

Lower than Craft.

Whitespace

Breathable.

Warm.

Shared.

Image Hierarchy

People first.

Textile second.

Typography

Quiet.

Editorial.

Supportive.

Never dominant.

Reading Tempo

Gentle.

Continuous.

The visitor should never feel encouraged to scroll faster.

Editorial Flow

Avoid compositions that visually reset.

Each composition should echo the position,
scale,
or rhythm
of the previous composition.

---

## Spatial Relationship Rule

Whitespace should connect compositions,
not separate them.

Distance represents emotional proximity.

Every large empty area should feel like shared space rather than absence.

---

# Narrative Frame One

## Presence

### Narrative Purpose

The visitor quietly meets another human before understanding anything about them.

Presence comes before explanation.

### Composition

A medium-scale environmental portrait.

Eye-level perspective.

One person is visible.

However, posture, gaze, or surrounding space should imply another unseen presence.

### Visual Weight

Moderate.

The image should invite attention without becoming monumental.

### Typography

A single reflective paragraph.

Positioned with generous distance from the image.

Reading should feel discovered rather than introduced.

Typography should never interrupt human relationships.

Whenever possible,

text observes the scene,

rather than dividing it.

### Atmosphere

Calm.

Warm.

Patient.

---

## Transition

### Presence → Connection

Do not create a visual reset.

The next relationship should begin appearing before Presence emotionally concludes.

The visitor should feel that another life quietly enters the same space.

---

# Narrative Frame Two

## Connection

### Narrative Purpose

Relationships become visible.

Not performance.

Not teaching.

Simply human connection.

### Composition

Two or more people share the frame.

Avoid perfect symmetry.

Distance between people becomes emotionally meaningful.

### Visual Weight

Slightly stronger than Frame One.

The relationship becomes the composition.

### Typography

Reflective rather than descriptive.

Encourage observation.

Never explanation.

### Atmosphere

Gentle.

Shared.

Natural.

---

## Transition

### Connection → Continuation

Without announcing change,

relationships slowly become shared actions.

Movement emerges naturally from togetherness.

---

# Narrative Frame Three

## Continuation

### Narrative Purpose

Reveal that culture survives through ordinary repetition.

No ceremony.

No spectacle.

Only everyday continuity.

### Composition

Fragments of interaction.

Hands.

Expressions.

Objects passing between people.

Shared attention.

Avoid staged storytelling.

### Visual Weight

Highest emotional density of the chapter.

Not necessarily the largest image.

Emotion carries the emphasis.

### Typography

Shortest copy within the chapter.

Images communicate most of the meaning.

### Atmosphere

Alive.

Warm.

Quietly hopeful.

---

## Transition

### Continuation → Invitation

Activity slowly dissolves into stillness.

Nothing stops.

Everything simply continues beyond the frame.

---

# Narrative Frame Four

## Invitation

### Narrative Purpose

Leave emotional space.

Never conclude.

Never summarize.

Never instruct.

### Composition

A quieter portrait or shared human moment.

Generous negative space.

The textile appears naturally within life,

never ceremonially displayed.

### Visual Weight

Light.

### Typography

One final reflection.

No moral.

No slogan.

No call-to-action.

### Atmosphere

Open.

Breathable.

Forward-looking.

---

# Chapter Ending

The chapter should not feel finished.

Instead,

it should feel unfinished in the most peaceful way.

The visitor should feel that life continues beyond the screen.

Hope should begin emotionally before it begins visually.

---

# Success Criteria

The visitor should finish the chapter feeling that they have met people,

not studied heritage.

The emotional handoff into Hope should happen almost invisibly.
