# Craft Layout Brief

Status: 🔒 Blueprint

This document translates the Craft Story Freeze into spatial, structural, and editorial decisions. It defines how the story of making occupies space. It intentionally avoids implementation details such as React, CSS, or GSAP.

---

## 1. Spatial Mission

To introduce humanity into the experience without breaking the calm established by Nature.

The space should feel inhabited, but never crowded.

The layout must communicate that craftsmanship is performed through patience rather than speed.

---

## 2. Spatial Emotion

Purposeful.

Grounded.

Rhythmic.

Every block should feel intentionally placed, like tools carefully arranged before work begins.

---

## 3. Vertical Rhythm

Unlike Nature's gradual descent, Craft introduces cadence.

The rhythm should resemble repeated human gestures.

Large moments.

↓

Small moments.

↓

Large moments.

↓

Pause.

↓

Repeat.

Scrolling should feel like following someone's hands through a familiar routine.

---

## 4. Section Density

Medium.

Denser than Hero.

More structured than Nature.

Less dense than Meaning.

Whitespace still exists, but now serves to separate actions instead of atmosphere.

---

## 5. Visual/Text Ratio

75% Visual

25% Text

The making process should always be observed before it is explained.

---

## 6. Reading Width

Maintain the editorial reading width established globally.

Text should never span excessively wide columns.

Reading should remain intimate.

---

## 7. Eye Path

The eye should move in a gentle zig-zag.

Unlike Nature's wandering "S" curve, Craft alternates between left and right in a predictable rhythm.

This mirrors repeated manual work.

---

## 8. Composition Principles

Hands dominate.

Tools support.

Materials connect them.

Typography observes.

Nothing should compete with the hands.

Large imagery should occasionally bleed outside the grid to preserve the feeling that work continues beyond the viewport.

---

## 9. Negative Space Strategy

Negative space represents pauses between actions.

It should feel intentional.

Not empty.

The user should experience moments of rest between repeated gestures.

---

## 10. Scale Strategy

Alternate scales frequently.

Large close-up.

↓

Small annotation.

↓

Medium composition.

↓

Large gesture.

Avoid repetitive card layouts.

Variation reinforces rhythm.

---

## 11. Editorial Principles

Observation before explanation.

Every image should communicate an action.

Typography explains why the action matters.

Captions behave like workshop notes rather than museum labels.

---

## 12. Transition from Nature

Nature concludes with untouched material.

Craft begins with the first deliberate human touch.

The transition should feel almost silent.

No dramatic visual break.

The first gesture should emerge naturally from the final stillness of Nature.

---

## 13. Transition into Meaning

The final layout should gradually become more ordered.

Loose fibers become organized.

Individual actions become complete sequences.

This visual organization prepares the user for the symbolic interpretation explored in Meaning.

---

## 14. Visual Tempo

Andante.

Steady.

Measured.

Never hurried.

Every major visual block should remain on screen long enough for the user to appreciate the physical gesture being depicted.

---

## 15. Human Review Checklist

Before implementation, the layout must satisfy:

- [ ] Do the hands naturally become the visual protagonist?
- [ ] Does the layout feel rhythmic instead of repetitive?
- [ ] Does every image communicate an action?
- [ ] Is typography always secondary to observation?
- [ ] Does whitespace feel like pauses between movements?
- [ ] Does the ending naturally prepare the user for Meaning?
