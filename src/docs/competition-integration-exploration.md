# Competition Integration Exploration
## The Living Threads

---

## Purpose

Following the completion of the documentary experience, the project must now be evaluated within the specific context of the SDGs Creative Web Competition.

The objective is not to redesign the experience.

Instead, it is to determine how the competition positioning can be communicated without compromising the editorial philosophy established throughout the project.

This document explores several possible approaches before selecting one implementation strategy.

---

# Core Question

How can visitors immediately recognize that they are experiencing the public-facing website of a digital cultural initiative while preserving the slow, observational nature of the documentary?

The solution must strengthen competition alignment without introducing conventional promotional behavior.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

Every explored approach will be evaluated against the following principles.

• Preserve the documentary narrative.

• Preserve emotional pacing.

• Maintain editorial restraint.

• Improve competition clarity.

• Avoid conventional landing-page patterns.

• Protect the Design Intent.

---

# Approach A
## Documentary First

### Description

Keep the website almost entirely unchanged.

Only add minimal contextual information outside the documentary itself.

The experience continues exactly as designed.

Visitors infer the initiative through subtle framing.

---

### Advantages

The documentary remains completely untouched.

Editorial immersion reaches its maximum.

No rhythm changes are introduced.

No visual language changes are required.

---

### Drawbacks

Competition judges may initially interpret the project as an editorial website instead of a landing page.

The digital solution becomes implicit rather than explicit.

---

### Alignment

Editorial Quality:
★★★★★

Competition Clarity:
★★☆☆☆

Risk:
Medium

---

# Approach B
## Framed Documentary

### Description

Introduce the identity of the initiative before the documentary begins.

The documentary itself remains structurally identical.

Instead of immediately entering the narrative, visitors first understand what they are about to experience.

After this brief introduction, the website transitions naturally into the existing Hero chapter.

Nothing inside the documentary changes.

---

### Advantages

Immediately communicates that this experience belongs to a digital initiative.

Improves branding.

Maintains documentary integrity.

Requires minimal implementation changes.

Preserves every existing chapter.

---

### Drawbacks

Requires careful copywriting.

The introduction must remain extremely restrained to avoid feeling promotional.

---

### Alignment

Editorial Quality:
★★★★★

Competition Clarity:
★★★★★

Risk:
Low

---

# Approach C
## Documentary With Closing Initiative

### Description

Maintain the existing opening exactly as it is.

Only reveal the initiative after Hope.

The documentary becomes the emotional foundation before introducing the broader mission.

---

### Advantages

Protects immersion completely.

Brand identity feels earned.

Provides a natural transition into future calls-to-action.

---

### Drawbacks

The initiative remains invisible during most of the experience.

Judges may not immediately recognize the project as a landing page.

Brand recognition arrives very late.

---

### Alignment

Editorial Quality:
★★★★★

Medium

# Editorial Analysis

The documentary has already reached production quality.

Therefore, introducing an entirely new chapter would unnecessarily disturb the carefully balanced emotional rhythm.

Similarly, inserting promotional content between existing chapters would interrupt the uninterrupted narrative progression established throughout the project.

The strongest strategy is therefore one that frames the documentary rather than modifying it.

The documentary itself should remain the central experience.

The surrounding context—not the narrative—should communicate the existence of the digital initiative.

---

# Recommendation

Approach B best satisfies both the Design Intent and the competition requirements.

Rather than changing the documentary, it simply provides visitors with the appropriate context before the experience begins.

The story remains identical.

The pacing remains identical.

The emotional arc remains identical.

Only the visitor's understanding changes.

This approach achieves the greatest increase in competition alignment while introducing the smallest possible disturbance to the editorial experience.
